MALDIVES

The Maldives, officially known as the Republic of Maldives, is a tropical paradise located in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India.
It is an island nation consisting of 26 atolls, which are made up of over 1,000 coral islands.

Here are some key facts and information about the Maldives:

  1. Tourism: The Maldives is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning beaches, overwater bungalows, water sports, snorkeling, and scuba diving. It's a top choice for honeymooners and travelers seeking a tropical paradise.
  2. Geography: The Maldives is the lowest-lying country in the world, with an average ground level of just 1.5 meters (4 feet 11 inches) above sea level. Due to its geography, it is highly vulnerable to rising sea levels caused by climate change.
  3. Economy: Tourism is the backbone of the Maldivian economy. The country is famous for its luxury resorts, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. Fishing is another important industry, particularly for the local population.
  4. Culture: The Maldives has a rich cultural heritage influenced by South Asian, Arab, and African cultures.
    Islam is the official religion, and the majority of the population is Muslim. Traditional music, dance, and art are important aspects of Maldivian culture.
  5. Environment: The Maldives is renowned for its biodiversity and underwater beauty. Its coral reefs are home to a wide variety of marine life, including colorful fish, sharks, and turtles. Conservation efforts are important to protect these fragile ecosystems.
